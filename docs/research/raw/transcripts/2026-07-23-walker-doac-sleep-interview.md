# Matthew Walker — DOAC sleep interview: extraction notes

## 1. The QQRT framework (the organizing spine)

Walker frames good sleep as a chair with **four legs — QQRT**:

- **Quantity** — enough sleep; the healthy adult band is a **7–9 hour range**, not a single number.
- **Quality** — continuity/efficiency of the sleep you get; the practical proxy is **sleep efficiency ≥ 85%** (asleep time ÷ time in bed).
- **Regularity** — same bed/wake times day to day. "Regularity is king."
- **Timing** — sleeping in phase with your own circadian preference (chronotype), not just any 8 hours.

His **"3 most impactful things to start tonight"**: **(1) digital detox** before bed, **(2) regularity**
(fixed wake/bed even on weekends), **(3) light** (dim the evening environment).

## 2. Regularity statistics (the headline evidence)

From a **UK Biobank** analysis (~**60,000** participants), most-regular vs least-regular sleepers:

- **−49% all-cause mortality**
- **−57% cardiometabolic disease**
- **−39% cancer mortality**

Crucially, **sleep regularity beat sleep quantity** as a predictor of mortality — i.e. *when* you
sleep consistently mattered more than raw hours. This is the empirical basis for "regularity is king."

## 3. Practical protocol (the buildable evening/night layer)

- **Wind-down / evening dim — T-90 min:** dim the environment to **below 30 lux** with warm/yellow
  light. In his study this was associated with **+18% REM sleep**. (Optional tip: a free phone
  lux-meter app to check the room.)
- **Bedroom temperature:** aim **~18 °C / 67–68 °F** — the body needs to drop core temperature to
  initiate and hold sleep.
- **The "20-minute rule" (get out of bed):** if you've been awake ~20 minutes, **leave the bed**,
  go to a dimly lit room, read/listen to something calm, and return **only when sleepy** — with **no
  countdown to sleep**. This breaks the learned bed = wakefulness association (a CBT-I mechanism).
- **"Don't check the clock":** clock-watching during a night waking drives anxiety and prolongs it.
- **Calm-down toolkit for 3am wakings:** slow breathing (e.g. in 5 / hold 6 / out 7), a body scan,
  guided meditation, and a vividly narrated **"4K mental walk"** (attributed to **Alison Harvey**) —
  a detailed imagined journey that occupies the mind and speeds the return to sleep.
- **Sleep banking:** before a *known* upcoming deficit (travel, on-call, an event, a new baby),
  **extend time-in-bed to ~10 h in the preceding days**. Banked sleep gave **~40% less cognitive
  impairment** under later deprivation (**Walter Reed / Balkin** work).

## 4. Motivation / education statistics (quotable "why bother")

- **Regularity** (most vs least regular, UK Biobank ~60k): **−49% all-cause mortality, −57%
  cardiometabolic disease, −39% cancer mortality**; regularity **beat quantity** for predicting mortality.
- **"The shorter your sleep, the shorter your life."**
- **Body composition:** underslept dieters lose **~70% of their weight as muscle**, not fat.
- **Genes:** short sleep distorts the expression of **711 genes**.
- **Appetite:** short sleep raises hunger **~30–40%** (leptin ↓, ghrelin ↑).
- **Brain clearance:** deep NREM runs the brain's **glymphatic** "power cleanse" (clears
  beta-amyloid / tau associated with Alzheimer's).
- **Mental health (the heavy clinical stats):** **< 6 h sleep → 100–150% higher suicidality**;
  **frequent nightmares → 800%** elevated risk — Walker calls nightmares a **biomarker / "canary."**

## 5. DEBUNKS (things he explicitly says do NOT work — build nothing on these)

- **Fixed 90-minute "smart wake" devices** — sleep cycles range **70–120 min**, so a fixed 90-min
  assumption is "nonsense."
- **"8 hours for everyone"** — it's a **7–9 h range**, individual.
- **Blue-light-blocking as the main lever** — the real problem is **engagement** ("bed rotting" /
  doom-scrolling), not the light *wavelength*; the famous iPad study **didn't replicate**.
- **Magnesium as a sleep aid** — common forms (oxide/citrate) **don't cross the blood-brain barrier**
  → "expensive urine"; only **L-threonate** or a true deficiency has a plausible case.
- **Melatonin as a daily supplement** — it's a **darkness *signal*, not a sleep generator**; only
  ~**3.4 min** faster onset; correct dose is **0.1–3 mg** (NOT 10–20 mg); use **only for jet lag /
  circadian-phase disorders**.
- **Counting sheep** — makes onset *worse*.
- **Ambien / Z-drugs / benzodiazepines** — "**sedation ≠ sleep**"; Ambien **cuts glymphatic
  clearance ~30–40%**.

## 6. Endorsed (narrow, with caveats)

- **Ashwagandha + phosphatidylserine** — for the **"tired but wired"** phenotype (lowers
  cortisol / sympathetic drive).
- **DORAs** (suvorexant / lemborexant / daridorexant) as the more naturalistic prescription option,
  and **CBT-I** as the first-line non-drug treatment — **clinical**, not app features (at most an
  education card).
