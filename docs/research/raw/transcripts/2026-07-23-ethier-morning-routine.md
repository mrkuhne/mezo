# Jeremy Ethier — morning-routine video: extraction notes

## The six recommendations (each with its claimed rationale)

1. **Circadian-aligned wake time** — wake at a consistent time in phase with your body clock.
   Rationale: a steady wake anchor stabilizes the whole day's rhythm (sleep pressure, hormones,
   training readiness) and is the lever the rest of the routine hangs off.

2. **Morning sunlight** — get bright outdoor light soon after waking.
   Rationale: morning light sets the circadian clock, boosts daytime alertness, and (via an earlier
   evening melatonin onset) supports falling asleep on time that night.

3. **Morning weigh-in** — step on the scale first thing, fasted, post-toilet.
   Rationale: this is the most *consistent* daily condition, so the trend line is the least noisy —
   the point is the moving trend, not any single reading.

4. **Caffeine timing / cutoff** — delay caffeine ~60–90 min after waking, and set a **daily cutoff**
   (the app uses **14:00**) so it clears before bed.
   Rationale: delaying avoids an afternoon crash tied to the adenosine/cortisol curve; the cutoff
   protects that night's sleep (caffeine's long half-life).

5. **Morning training** — train in the morning when it fits.
   Rationale: morning exercise reinforces the wake anchor, improves adherence/consistency, and can
   aid nighttime sleep — a schedule/consistency argument, not a claim that muscle only grows AM.

6. **Protein-rich breakfast** — front-load protein at the first meal.
   Rationale: an early protein feeding supports muscle-protein synthesis after the overnight fast and
   improves satiety across the morning.
